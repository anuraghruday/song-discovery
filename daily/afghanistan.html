Walter Ferguson: The Calypso King of Costa Rica

"Mr. Gavitt" – A Legacy of Laughter and Life

The Uncrowned King of Cahuita

Walter Ferguson Byfield, born in Panama in 1919, became the undisputed "King of Calypso" in Costa Rica after his family settled in the charming fishing village of Cahuita. A self-taught maestro, he mastered the dulzaina, harmonica, ukulele, guitar, and his beloved clarinet, all without formal training. His music, a vibrant testament to Afro-Caribbean culture, intricately wove the daily joys and challenges of the Afro-Costa Rican community into unforgettable melodies.

Ferguson's compositions, estimated at over two hundred, are a unique blend of humor and tragedy, delivered in the distinctive local Creole English. His inspiration drawn from calypso legends like Mighty Sparrow, he crafted a legacy that resonated across generations until his peaceful passing in 2023 at the remarkable age of 103.

Walter Ferguson Byfield in Cahuita, 2017. Photo by Rodolfo Mora Vargas (CC BY-SA 4.0).

"Cabin in the Wata": A Whimsical Narrative

"Cabin in the Wata" stands as one of Ferguson's most cherished works, a playful narrative that epitomizes his storytelling prowess. The song playfully recounts the tale of Bato, an audacious builder who constructs a cabin directly in the water. The ensuing discovery by the "mistress of the National Park" and her demand for its immediate removal forms the humorous core of this calypso gem.

The song's enduring appeal lies in its vivid imagery, the clever use of language, and its lighthearted portrayal of a unique local dispute, all set to an infectious calypso beat.

Cahuita: Home of the Calypso King

Explore the coastal village where Walter Ferguson spent his life, composing and performing his legendary calypso tunes.

Ferguson's legacy extends far beyond his own performances. His work has been widely interpreted by other Costa Rican artists, ensuring that his unique calypso sound continues to thrive. Recognized with the "Patrimonio Cultural Inmaterial 'Emilia Prieto Tugores'" prize in 2017, his influence is further cemented by the annual Walter Ferguson International Calypso Festival established in Cahuita in 2013, celebrating the enduring spirit of his music.

Afghanistan: Where Ancient Echoes Meet Enduring Spirit

Afghanistan: Land of Ancient Crossroads and Resilient Culture

A Tapestry of History, Art, and Unyielding Spirit

The Heart of the Silk Road

Afghanistan, a landlocked country at the crossroads of Central and South Asia, boasts a history stretching back over 50,000 years. Its strategic location made it a vital artery of the ancient Silk Road, connecting diverse empires and cultures. This confluence shaped a unique heritage, evident in its rich archaeological sites, diverse languages, and spiritual traditions. From the towering Hindu Kush mountains to the fertile river valleys, Afghanistan's landscape is as dramatic and varied as its history.

Despite centuries of conflict, the Afghan people have maintained an unwavering spirit, deeply rooted in traditions of hospitality, poetry, and intricate craftsmanship. This resilience is a hallmark of a culture that has continuously adapted, preserved, and reinvented itself through the ages.

Dramatic landscape of Afghanistan's Hindu Kush region. Photo by Sana Akram on Unsplash.

Melodies of the Mountains: Rubab and Poetry

Music and poetry are the soul of Afghanistan. The **Rubab**, a short-necked plucked lute, is considered the national instrument, producing hauntingly beautiful melodies that are central to classical, folk, and contemporary Afghan music. Alongside instruments like the Dutar and Tanbur, the Rubab carries the weight of centuries-old oral traditions and intricate musical forms.

Afghan culture is also inextricably linked with poetry. The Persian language, spoken by many Afghans, has gifted the world figures like **Rumi**, whose mystical verses on love and spirituality continue to inspire globally. Oral storytelling, often accompanied by music, remains a cherished practice, ensuring that historical epics, folk tales, and personal narratives are passed down through generations, embodying the enduring spirit of the Afghan people.

In their contrasting beauty, they reveal a shared essence: the innate drive to make sense of our world, celebrate our experiences, and pass on our legacies through the timeless arts.
